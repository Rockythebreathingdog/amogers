We're anxious because of homework, because of others, man... we're even anxious if the food we're eating is gluten-free. Mark, you're not alone. The thing is, anxiety will always stay with us, but it doesn't mean we can't control it.

I used to feel the same way back then. I remember when I had to give a speech for a contest; it was the most nerve-racking experience. The judges were not smiling, and my competitors looked like they were about to demolish me. While I was sweating like crazy and just wanted to run away. But then a voice came and said "you're Martin Luther King." And I kept telling myself that I was him. And when it was my time to perform my speech, I walked to the stage like I was Martin Luther King and I spoke every word like I was Martin Luther King. I faked everything. You are probably thinking if I won first place or not. Well, no, I was close to last. But I learned one important lesson that day: I could just fake it until I make it. Every time I would talk to strangers I would tell myself I'm James Bond, every time I wanted to speak to my crush, I told myself I'm Tom Cruise. It worked like magic; you should try it.

We are anxious beings, but it doesn't mean we can't control how we feel. Sometimes you'd just have to say "I'm Tom Cruise" or "I'm Martin Luther King." Mark, sometimes you'd have to fake it and once you get used to it, you'll forget that you were pretending.
